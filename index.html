Why Do You Need eUSD?

Stable & Safe

eUSD stability is maintained through a combination of overcollateralization, liquidation mechanisms, and arbitrage opportunities. These factors work together to ensure that the value of eUSD remains close to its 1 USD peg.

Interest Bearing

Holders of eUSD can expect to earn a base annual percentage yield (APY) of approximately 7.2%. This stable yield is attractive for investors seeking a steady income stream while maintaining exposure to the cryptocurrency market. Moreover, as the price of ETH increases, the yield generated by eUSD holders may also rise.

0 Mint/Loan Cost

Lybra Finance stands out from other stablecoin protocols by offering 0 minting fee and 0 loan interest for users. This feature allows users to leverage their ETH holdings and mint eUSD stablecoins without incurring any additional costs.

Lybra DAO

Lybra DAO serves as the backbone of Lybra Finance's decision-making process.

The Lybra DAO will be governed by LBR token holders who will have the power to influence the direction of the project, propose and vote on various decisions, and collectively manage the protocol. This community-driven approach helps ensure that the Lybra Finance project remains true to its decentralized nature and that its development is in line with the interests of its users.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How can holders of stablecoins like USDT, USDC, and FRAX earn interest?

It's very simple. All you need to do is exchange your USDT for eUSD on a DEX. As long as you hold eUSD, Lybra will automatically calculate interest for you.

2. How is eUSD 1:1 hard pegged to 1 USD?

When eUSD price above 1 USD: If the eUSD price exceeds 1 USD, users can mint new eUSD by depositing ETH as collateral and then sell the newly minted eUSD on DEX. As more eUSD is sold, the market supply increases, pushing the price back down to 1 USD. Users can then buy back eUSD at a lower price or use it to repay their loans, realizing a profit from the price difference.

When eUSD price below 1 USD: If the eUSD price falls below 1 USD, users can purchase eUSD at a discounted rate on the market and then redeem it within the Lybra Protocol for $1 worth of ETH/stETH. As users buy up the undervalued eUSD, demand increases, driving the price back up to 1 USD. Users can either hold the redeemed ETH/stETH or sell it, profiting from the price difference.

3. How can I mint (borrow) with Lybra?

To mint (borrow) eUSD, you must deposit a certain amount of collateral (ETH or stETH) through Lybra.

Then you can mint eUSD against your collateral up to a collateral ratio of 170%.

4. Where does the interest on eUSD come from?

The interest on eUSD comes from the Lybra Protocol's interaction with stETH and Liquidity Staking Derivatives (LSD). When users deposit ETH as collateral, it is converted to stETH through the Lybra Protocol. The stETH earns yield from staking on the Ethereum 2.0 network, and this yield is further boosted by interacting with LSD.

The earned yield is then converted back into eUSD on a regular basis through decentralized exchanges (DEXs). As a result, eUSD holders receive a stable interest, with the base APY being around 8%. This interest is generated from the yield earned on the stETH and LSD mechanisms within the Lybra Protocol.

5. Does Lybra charge any fees?

There are no minting fees or borrowing interest on Lybra Protocol.

6. How can I earn money using Lybra?

There are four different ways to generate revenue using Lybra:

  • Deposit ETH and mint eUSD to earn yield in eUSD and rewards in LBR.
  • Provide eUSD/ETH LP to earn LBR.
  • Hold LBR to share Lybra Protocol revenue.
  • Become a Liquidator or Liquidation Keeper to earn ETH.

7. It seems the eUSD yield is lower than stETH. Why would I put my ETH on to Lybra, instead of other LSD protocols?

While it's true that eUSD yield might be lower than stETH or other LSD protocols, there are several reasons why someone might choose to deposit their ETH into Lybra Finance:

1. Interest-bearing stablecoin: By depositing ETH into Lybra, users can mint eUSD, an interest-bearing stablecoin, which allows them to earn a stable income on their holdings. This can be particularly attractive for those who want to reduce exposure to the volatile nature of the cryptocurrency market while still earning a passive income.

2. Leveraged long on ETH: Lybra Finance allows users to deposit ETH as collateral and mint eUSD without any borrowing fees or interest. Users can use the minted eUSD to purchase more ETH, effectively going leveraged long on ETH with zero cost. This strategy can be beneficial in a bullish market, as it allows users to potentially increase their returns while maintaining exposure to ETH.

3. Decentralization and stability: Lybra Finance accepts only ETH and stETH as collateral, making it more decentralized and stable compared to other stablecoin platforms that accept multiple types of collateral. This can be an important factor for users who prioritize decentralization and the long-term stability of the platform.

4. Token incentives: Lybra Finance offers additional incentives in the form of LBR tokens for users who deposit ETH and mint eUSD. These tokens can be earned as rewards, giving users an extra layer of income generation beyond the stable interest offered by eUSD. LBR tokens also grant holders governance rights, allowing them to participate in the decision-making process within the Lybra DAO. This can be appealing for users who want to actively engage in the platform's development and growth while also benefiting from the token's potential appreciation. Ultimately, the choice to deposit ETH into Lybra Finance or other LSD protocols depends on an individual's investment goals, risk tolerance, and preference for stability versus potential yield.
